A game designed to teach principles of bank risk management.

How to play

Each player is a bank. Every round, players choose their deposit interest rates, allocate capital to a chosen portfolio of bonds, risky loans, and hedge instruments (swaps and options). Players that choose a high deposit interest rate gain a larger share of total deposits, but pay higher interest expense. Players who allocate their capital to long-term (short-term) bonds expose themselves to higher duration risk. Players who allocate to risky loans earn higher expected interest income, but expose themselves to default risk. Players who hedge can reduce their downside risk, but must pay a premium and/or limit their upside gains. The object of the game is to survive (avoid a bank run) by maintaining positive and growing equity, fulfill capital requirements, maximize profit, and effectively control risks. Students learn about interest rate risk and other principles of banking by playing to win against their peers. The Central Bank sets short rates and capital requirements.

The balance sheet

Assets = Cash + Bonds + Loans, funded by Deposits + Equity. Equity is a real capital base that absorbs losses and is carried across rounds — your score is your equity level, and the capital ratio (Equity / Assets) decides whether you fulfill capital requirements and survive.

The change-in-equity identity

ΔEquity = Net Interest Income + Securities Gains/Losses + Hedge P&L − Credit losses − Fire-sale losses in the event of low equity

Classroom flow

Set economy & modules → players make decisions, enter decisions → Lock decisions → generate the new interest rates each round → Resolve balance sheet and incomes → Scoreboard → next round. Each round's new rates become the next round's starting curve.

Engine audited against the original Excel: with a flat curve, bond prices, capital gains and the core P&L match to the cent.
